Policy Reference Document
=========================
Policy Name: US Outbound Investment Screening (EO 14105 and COINS Act)
Date Enacted: 2023-08-09
Issuing Authority: Executive Office of the President (US) and US Congress
Policy Type: Investment Screening
Source URL: https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/international/outbound-investment-program

Industries Affected: Technology, Enterprise Software & Semiconductors; Financial & Professional Services
Firms Affected: Intel; NVIDIA; Qualcomm; JPMorgan Chase; Goldman Sachs; BlackRock; Citigroup

Key Provisions:
First-ever US program to screen and restrict outbound American investments into China, addressing intangible benefits of investment (know-how transfer, talent networks, market access). EO 14105 signed August 9, 2023; final Treasury rule published October 28, 2024; Outbound Investment Security Program (OISP) became operational January 2, 2025. The COINS Act, signed December 18, 2025, codified the program with statutory authority. Covers three critical technology sectors: semiconductors and microelectronics, quantum information technologies, and artificial intelligence. Transactions are divided into prohibited (advanced integrated circuits, quantum computing, military-applicable AI) and notifiable categories. Civil penalties reach up to $377,700 per violation or twice the transaction amount.

Strategic Implication:
Directly constrains US financial firms and investors from deploying capital into Chinese semiconductor, quantum, and AI sectors. BlackRock and other asset managers must screen China-focused portfolios for prohibited and notifiable transactions.
