Policy Reference Document
=========================
Policy Name: China NEV Dual-Credit Policy
Date Enacted: 2017-09-27
Issuing Authority: Ministry of Industry and Information Technology of China
Policy Type: Industrial Policy
Source URL: https://www.iea.org/policies/14779-dual-credit-system

Industries Affected: Automotive, EV & Advanced Manufacturing
Firms Affected: Tesla; BMW Group; Volkswagen Group; Mercedes-Benz Group; Toyota; Stellantis; Bosch

Key Provisions:
Requires passenger car manufacturers and importers to meet both Corporate Average Fuel Consumption (CAFC) targets and New Energy Vehicle (NEV) credit quotas. NEV credit ratios escalated from 10% (2019) to 18% (2023) to 38% (2025) to 48% (2026) to 58% (2027). Surplus NEV credits can offset CAFC deficits but CAFC credits cannot offset NEV deficits. Major amendments in June 2020 (Phase 2), July 2023 (Phase 3), and November 2025 (Phase 4).

Strategic Implication:
Tesla benefits as a net credit seller, while foreign joint ventures (VW, Toyota) face deficits forcing accelerated NEV production or credit purchases. NEV penetration surged past 40% by 2024-2025, intensifying pressure on lagging foreign OEMs.
