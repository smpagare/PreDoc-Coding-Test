Policy Reference Document
=========================
Policy Name: BIS Entity List Expansions (China)
Date Enacted: 2019-2024
Issuing Authority: US Bureau of Industry and Security
Policy Type: Trade Controls & Export Restrictions
Source URL: https://www.bis.gov/press-release/commerce-further-restricts-chinas-artificial-intelligence-advanced-computing-capabilities

Industries Affected: Technology, Enterprise Software & Semiconductors
Firms Affected: Intel; NVIDIA; Qualcomm; Apple (indirect via suppliers)

Key Provisions:
Progressive restriction tool requiring US government licenses before any US-origin items can be exported to listed entities. Key milestones: Huawei added May 2019; SMIC added December 2020; landmark semiconductor export controls issued October 7, 2022 (restricting advanced computing chips, semiconductor manufacturing equipment, and US person support for Chinese chip development); loopholes tightened October 2023; 140 entities added December 2024. As of March 2025, over 1,065 Chinese entities were on the Entity List, nearly one-third of all entries.

Strategic Implication:
NVIDIA's China revenue dropped from 26% to ~13% of total with a $5.5 billion inventory charge in 2025; semiconductor firms face progressive market access loss. Huawei developed domestic alternatives, demonstrating limits of export controls.
