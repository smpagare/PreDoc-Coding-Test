Policy Reference Document
=========================
Policy Name: US CHIPS and Science Act
Date Enacted: 2022-08-09
Issuing Authority: US Congress
Policy Type: Industrial Policy
Source URL: https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2022/08/09/fact-sheet-chips-and-science-act-will-lower-costs-create-jobs-strengthen-supply-chains-and-counter-china/

Industries Affected: Technology, Enterprise Software & Semiconductors
Firms Affected: Intel; NVIDIA; Qualcomm

Key Provisions:
Landmark legislation (P.L. 117-167) authorizing approximately $280 billion in new funding, with $52.7 billion specifically appropriated for semiconductor manufacturing, R&D, and workforce development. The $39 billion Manufacturing Incentive Fund provides direct subsidies for fab construction. Guardrails prohibit recipients from expanding advanced chip capacity in China through 10-year guardrails. Major awards include: TSMC Arizona ($6.6 billion direct plus $5 billion in loans for three fabs), Intel ($7.86 billion plus $3 billion for a Secure Enclave), Samsung ($4.745 billion), Micron ($6.165 billion), and GlobalFoundries ($1.5 billion). As of July 2025, $30.9 billion had been awarded across 40 projects.

Strategic Implication:
Intel benefits from direct manufacturing subsidies but 10-year guardrails prohibit advanced chip capacity expansion in China, creating an explicit trade-off between US subsidies and China market access for semiconductor firms.
